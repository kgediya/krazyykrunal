For My Priye

A sorry from the deepest part of my heart.

This is not to excuse what I did. It is to own it fully, tell you what I understand now, and remind you that my love for you is bigger than my ego, my habits, or one bad night.

Priye, I am truly sorry.

Last night I made a sarcastic and insensitive joke, and that alone was hurtful. But what hurts me even more to realize is that I let you carry that feeling into sleep. You deserved comfort, softness, and reassurance from me, not a wound to sit with.

I also want to own something deeper about me. I have a habit of dragging past topics back into disagreements, arguments, and even jokes. I use it like a defense mechanism to protect myself, to feel like I have the upper hand, and sometimes to make the other person feel guilty. That is unfair, immature, and damaging to the bond we are trying to protect.

You are not my opponent. You are my person. I never want my words to become a place where you feel punished instead of loved.

I am sorry for the joke. I am sorry for reopening old things. I am sorry for letting my defensiveness speak louder than my love. And I am sorry that you had to sleep with that heaviness. The truth is, it scares me to think that my own actions could ever be the reason I hurt you enough to lose you. That fear does not come from possession. It comes from knowing how deeply I love you and how badly I never want to become careless with your heart.

I love you to infinity and beyond, Priye.
More gently. More honestly. More carefully. Always.
